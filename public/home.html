The Problem
The industry has never had a standard way to score mechanical condition.

Facility owners and property managers receive service reports after every maintenance visit. Those reports range from a single line — "unit good" — to pages of technical jargon written for engineers, not owners. Even the most detailed report leaves the client asking the same question: what is the actual condition of my plant or building?

There is no standardized scoring framework. No weighted methodology that accounts for asset criticality, physical condition, and operational data together. The result is deferred maintenance, surprise failures, and million-dollar decisions made on gut feel.

Over time this information gap has created a deeper problem — eroding trust between facility owners and the contractors they depend on. Without objective data, clients cannot verify the quality of service they receive.
