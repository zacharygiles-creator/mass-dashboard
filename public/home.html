The Problem
If you've ever written "unit good" and known it wasn't the whole story — this was built for you.

Every refrigeration and HVAC contractor in the country faces the same problem. The technician performs the inspection. Writes up findings — if they write them up at all. Hands the report to a service coordinator who passes it to an account manager who's supposed to quote the work. Somewhere in that chain the information dies.

The account manager is quoting blind. The customer is making million-dollar decisions with no objective data. Upper management is approving maintenance budgets based on gut feel and vague service reports. And the technician — the one person who actually knows what's happening in that machine room — has no way to communicate what they saw in a language that moves anyone to action.

This isn't a people problem. It's a systems problem. There has never been a standard way to score, communicate, and trend the condition of a refrigeration or HVAC plant. Until now.

The Solution
Two scores. One number. Built on 30 years of field experience in the most complex mechanical environment in existence.

MASSCORE is a proprietary mechanical assessment scoring system developed by industrial ammonia refrigeration technicians. If the methodology holds up in an ammonia machine room — it holds up anywhere. Every asset class. Every failure mode. Every reading that matters.

MASS
Plant Integrity Score
M
Mechanical
Built for ammonia refrigeration, chiller plants, cold storage, and commercial HVAC. Every asset class. Every failure mode that matters in the field.
A
Assessment
A structured, rubric-driven assessment methodology that produces consistent scores regardless of which technician performs it. The system removes subjectivity.
S
Scoring
Every asset scored by its physical condition — stripped of serviceable components. What is the irreversible structural state of this equipment right now?
S
System
Asset class weighting, criticality-based rollups, service life status, and prognosis logic — working as a complete system to produce the Plant Integrity Score.
The Plant Integrity Score answers one question: what is the ceiling of this asset's potential regardless of how well it is maintained? It degrades with age, repair history, and structural deterioration. It cannot be recovered by replacing components.
CORE
Plant Health Score
C
Condition
Component health. Compressor readings. Oil temperatures, filter differentials, amp draw — every operational parameter that reflects current condition.
O
Operations
How well is this plant being maintained right now? The Plant Health Score responds to service work — fix it, the score rises. Defer it, the score falls.
R
Reliability
Open findings apply score penalties by severity. Resolve them — the score recovers. The roadmap tells you exactly which repairs move the score the most.
E
Engine
The intelligence layer. The algorithm that converts 30 years of field expertise into a number that a plant manager, a CFO, and a technician can all act on.
The Plant Health Score answers one question: how well is this facility being maintained right now? It's the score that responds to your team's work — and the score that justifies the next service contract renewal.
MASSCORE — The methodology and the engine. The asset and its condition. Two scores — one truth.
